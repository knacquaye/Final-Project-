In the heart of San Francisco, a vibrant city known for its iconic landmarks and cultural diversity, there lies a complex landscape shaped by varying degrees of crime. This project aims to delve deep into the city's crime statistics, offering residents, policymakers, law enforcement, and researchers an in-depth look at the patterns, trends, and hotspots of crime within the city

    • Methodology

    • Introduction

      This section outlines the methodology followed in the development of the crime data visualization website, detailing the design, development, and data handling

    • Project Overview

      The website aims to provide a visual representation of crime dynamics in San Francisco, helping users identify patterns and trends in criminal activities.

    • Development Process

      Front-end Development: I used a template from https://html5up.net/stellar as my website interface

    • Data Handling

      Data Sources: The crime data was sourced from San Francisco Police Department(SFPD).

      Data Processing: The data was cleaned and structured using MS Excel before visualization.

      Data Visualization: D3.js was used to create all visualisations and charts.

    • Limitations and Future Work

      Limitations: The visualization is limited to the data provided in the CSV file.

      Future Enhancements: Future work includes adding more detailed crime data, such as time of day and location(neighbourhoods), and enhancing interactivity.
